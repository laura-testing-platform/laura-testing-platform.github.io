How did I get here? From HR (People) Professional to Career and Wellness Coach

Skimming past school – university - a masters etc, I have spent the best part of ten years working in HR when I was introduced to coaching. Fast-forward another year and I qualified with two diplomas: Personal Performance Coaching, and Corporate and Executive Coaching.

Having worked in HR across the creative sector, with a focus on L&D and wellbeing, I have knowledge and experience in these areas, and more importantly, I feel passionate about them! Coaching is the way that I can use my passion and invest it into a role where I can help others find what they love to do too.

Still reading? A few more things that you might find interesting about me..

  • I love to empower others +

      Having come out of coaching sessions myself with feelings of excitement, planning, and positivity – I know how good it feels! And to be able to support others in this way is what I am really passionate about. People who are go-getters, who don’t want to settle, and who want to feel empowered.

  • I’m a list-lover +

      Yes, I have to admit it, you will always find me writing to-do lists... It’s what makes me feel organised and capable to flex my day or week by having something visual on some paper. It’s what works for me!

  • I came close to burnout +

      In 2016. Despite thinking I had a grip on my work-life balance, I did not. It’s easy to miscalculate the realities of everyday life and manage your expectations of yourself. But having worked with a Coach and reassessed my priorities, I now know what keeps me well and in balance.

  • I used to find it hard to believe in myself +

      I am definitely an ambivert. Growing up, I had times where I doubted myself and my abilities. It has taken time to become more confident and proud of myself and my achievements. Having developed myself in this way I know how to help others with insecurities.

  • Hablo español +

      After growing up in the countryside I went to uni in the South West to do a degree in Spanish, this is when I spent a year living and working in Barcelona. Hands-down one of the best years of my life so far!

  • Always the optimist +

      If you think the glass is half empty, we will probably find it hard to work together! For me challenges are opportunities, they are possibilities that will provide us with experiences to learn from. It’s time to see things for the journey that they will take us on for our own self-development.

  • I have my own Coach too +

      Hell yes! If you work with a Coach who doesn’t have their own Coach, then fool on you.. To be able to ‘walk my talk’ I receive coaching to keep me on track, to be able to offer the best coaching I can to you and to ensure I am continually growing and developing.

  • My coaching style +

      Although I am described as quite calm and measured, my approach is tailored to each person I work with. I see coaching as working in partnership, you don’t need to work on your goals alone. I will support you, remind you of your progress and celebrate your success.
      Everyone has other things going on, so we will balance the realities of the day-to-day, so that you can stay focused on your goals. As long as you are 100% committed to the coaching process, good things will happen. Together we will help you find fulfilment and be the best version of yourself.

  • Who have I worked with? +

      Over the last ten years I have worked in retail, advertising and executive search with lots of different people. My coaching experience lies at middle and senior management level, as well as 1-2-1 private clients of differing ages, careers, and goals.
